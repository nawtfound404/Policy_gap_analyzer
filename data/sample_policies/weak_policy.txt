Information Security Policy

Version: 1.0
Approved By: Executive Management
Applies To: Entire Organization

1. Purpose

This policy establishes mandatory requirements for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information assets. It defines governance expectations, responsibilities, and control requirements to manage cybersecurity risk across the organization.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all systems, applications, networks, data, employees, contractors, and third parties that access or manage organizational information assets.

3. Roles and Responsibilities

Executive Management shall provide oversight and ensure enforcement of this policy.

The Information Security Officer is responsible for defining, maintaining, and monitoring security controls.

System Owners shall be accountable for protecting assets under their ownership.

All personnel must comply with this policy and report security incidents immediately.

4. Asset Management

The organization shall maintain an asset inventory that identifies all information systems, hardware, software, and data assets.
Each asset must have an assigned owner responsible for its protection and lifecycle management.
Assets shall be classified based on sensitivity and criticality to determine appropriate security controls.

5. Risk Management

The organization shall conduct risk assessments at regular intervals to identify threats, vulnerabilities, and potential impacts.
Risk tolerance and acceptance decisions must be documented and approved by management.
Identified risks shall be treated through mitigation, transfer, acceptance, or avoidance.

6. Access Control

Access to information systems shall be restricted based on the principle of least privilege.
User access must be authorized, authenticated, and reviewed periodically.
Privileged access shall be limited to authorized personnel and monitored for misuse.

7. Data Security

Information shall be classified according to sensitivity and business impact.
Sensitive data must be protected using encryption when stored or transmitted.
Data handling procedures shall ensure protection against unauthorized disclosure or modification.

8. Security Awareness and Training

All personnel must receive security awareness training at least annually.
Training shall cover responsibilities, acceptable use, and incident reporting procedures.

9. Continuous Monitoring and Logging

The organization shall implement logging and monitoring to detect security events.
Logs must be reviewed regularly to identify anomalous or suspicious activity.

10. Incident Response

The organization shall maintain an incident response process for detecting, reporting, and managing security incidents.
Incident response roles, escalation paths, and communication procedures must be defined and tested periodically.

11. Recovery and Continuity

The organization shall maintain a recovery plan to restore systems and data following incidents.
Backups must be performed regularly and restoration procedures shall be tested.
Post-incident reviews shall be conducted to identify improvements.

12. Policy Review and Enforcement

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually and updated as needed.
Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action in accordance with organizational procedures.
